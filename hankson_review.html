Section 01

Used To

Past habits that are no longer true. Watch out for the "greedy verb" trap.

Quick Reference

Past habit
主詞 + used to + 原形動詞
過去經常…… (but no longer true)
My dad used to record her singing. → he doesn't anymore
Present habit / accustomed to
主詞 + be/get used to + N / V-ing
習慣…… (current, ongoing)
My dad is used to recording her singing. → he does it regularly now
Negative
主詞 + didn't use to + 原形動詞
Jeff didn't use to take pictures with his phone.
Question
Did + 主詞 + use to + 原形動詞?
Did you use to make calls with LINE?
💡 The Greedy Verb Rule: When "didn't" or "Did" enters the sentence, it steals the past tense — drop the "d" from "used". used to → use to.

⚠️ Key difference: used to + V (past, finished) vs. be used to + V-ing (present habit). The "-ing" is your signal it's a present habit!

Section 02

Comparatives & Superlatives

Comparing two things vs. the best/worst of a group. Watch for "than" and "the".

Quick Reference

Comparativeadjective + -er / more + adj + than
Superlativethe + adj + -est / the most + adj
As … asTed is as healthy as Daniel. (equal)
Trap ⚠️See "than"? → comparative. See "of all"? → superlative.
💡 The "than" rule: If you see "than" in the sentence, you MUST use a comparative — never a superlative. "The most" never goes with "than".
